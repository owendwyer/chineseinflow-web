Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

This policy describes how Chinese In Flow (the website at chineseinflow.com and the Android app) handles information. Owen Dwyer operates this site and app.

Summary

  • The Android app does not connect to the internet and does not send data to us.
  • The website game sends anonymous usage statistics so we can see how the game is used.
  • We do not sell your data, run ads, or require an account.

Android app

The Chinese In Flow Android app (Google Play) stores your study progress and settings in a local database on your device. The app does not request network access and does not transmit your progress or personal information to us or third parties.

If you tap Rate this app, your device opens the Google Play Store in a separate app — that is handled by Google, not by Chinese In Flow.

Android may back up app data to your Google account if you have device backup enabled. That backup is managed by Google under your account settings.

Website

When you play the game on this website, the browser loads game assets from our hosting provider (Netlify). Standard web server logs may include your IP address, browser type, and pages visited. We do not use those logs to identify individual users.

Anonymous usage statistics

The web game sends anonymous usage events to our metrics service (gtlemetrics.co.uk). These events help us understand whether the game loads correctly and how it is used. Typical events include page load, page close, and load failures.

Data sent may include:

  • A random session identifier (not linked to your name or account)
  • Screen orientation and display scale
  • Event type and timestamp

We do not collect your name, email, precise location, or payment information through the game. The website game does not use cookies for tracking or advertising.

Information you send us

If you contact us, any information you choose to include in your message (such as your email address) is used only to respond to you.

Children

Chinese In Flow is an educational tool. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children through the app or website game beyond the anonymous usage statistics described above.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page shows when it was last revised.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Get in touch.
